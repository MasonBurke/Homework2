About me

While growing up I've always had a creative mind that loved problem solving and a drive to do things differently than others. Fast forward to my early teens during the big craze of MySpace I had my first taste of coding, I didn't even have an idea of what the process was called I just had so much fun figuring out what meant what in the text and how to make my profile look cooler than my peers did.

Now lets fast forward a bit more, I'm living in a self converted tiny home on wheels and I had just moved to Denver. I'm feeling a bit lost in life and find out theres a coding boot camp coming up in the area, I make the commitment and enroll then boom. The coronavirus pandemic happens. Every coffee shop and public work space has to close it's doors, the little home I built out was designed as minimalist as possible and I did not include any sufficient charging station or source of wi-fi. As I expected the course to be at the university I didn't think I'd need any of these things so everyday has been an adversity in itself - hot spot issues, running out of data, getting moved on from places I've parked my home and laptops dying but I know I'm someone who commits and perseveres.

I'm one month into this three month coding boot camp now and things are going a bit more smoothly now but I think this is a test on me shows what I'm willing to go through more than some and I hope I can show that through my work.
